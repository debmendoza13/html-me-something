My Florida Vacation

Hi! My name is Deb. I just returned from a trip to Ft. Lauderdale, Florida. I travelled with my boyfriend, my sister, and her daughter and two grandchildren. We spent time with my niece who lives in Miami. We visited the Keys and the Southenmost Point. We went on an airboat tour and got to see crocodiles in the Everglades. We took a boat tour in Miami to see celebrity homes, the Miami skyline at night, and Star Island. We ate the best roasted chicken I've ever had at a Cuban restaurant off of Ocean Drive in South Beach. We spent part of our last day at the beach.

The Florida Keys

The Keys are a string of islands that stretch over 120 miles south of the coast of Florida into the Atlantic Ocean and Gulf of Mexico. The islands are connected by U.S. Highway 1, the only road between Key Largo and Key West. Key West is the southernmost city in the Keys, famous for the Southernmost Point, Hemingway's House, and Truman's Little White House.

The Everglades

The Everglades National Park spans 1.5 million acres and covers the majority of southern Florida. It is a wetlands preserve that is home to hundreds of animal species including the endangered leatherback turtle, Florida panther, and West Indian manatee. The Everglades is also home to plants and trees that do not grow anywhere else in the U.S. It is also the largest remaining subtropical wilderness in the U.S.

While in the Everglades we visited Gator Park, which offered airboat rides to glimpse alligators in the wild. (We saw two!) After the airboat ride, we saw a presentation about alligators and crocodiles, with real alligators and crocodiles. I even got to hold a baby gator!

Miami

There are several things to do in Miami. Boat tours, bus tours, museums, Ocean Drive, restaurants, the beach, etc. We chose the Historic Miami Skyline Tour and Homes of the Rich & Famous Sightseeing Cruise, a relaxing 90-minute boat tour with beautiful views of the Miami skyline at night. We also got to see Star Island, an exclusive island home to celebrities. There is one road to the island with a security gate and guest list. The island has its own police department and banks.

We spent an afternoon at South Miami Beach across from Ocean Drive, a two-lane road with colorful hotels, condos, and restaurants. It sprinkled briefly and scared many people off the beach. It was a windy day at the beach, but otherwise the weather was mild and warm.
